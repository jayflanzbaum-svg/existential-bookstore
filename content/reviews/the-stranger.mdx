The Stranger remains the most perfectly constructed novel of the twentieth century. In barely a hundred pages, [Camus](/authors/camus) delivers the full weight of existential philosophy through the simple story of a man who refuses to play by society's emotional rules. Meursault's honesty is terrifying because it holds up a mirror to our own performed feelings. The sun-drenched prose of Algeria makes the darkness beneath all the more unsettling.
