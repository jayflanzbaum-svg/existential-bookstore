Salman [Rushdie](/authors/rushdie) took the myth of Orpheus and Eurydice—the musician who descends into the underworld to retrieve his lost love—and rebuilt it as a sweeping novel about rock and roll, exile, and the collision of East and West in the twentieth century. The Ground Beneath Her Feet is one of the most ambitious novels I have ever read, and one of the most rewarding. Rushdie writes with a density and allusiveness that demands your full attention, but he earns it on every page. The central love story is genuinely devastating, and the rock music mythology he constructs is brilliantly executed. This is a book about the things we lose when we cross borders, the things we carry that we cannot name, and the music that holds us together when nothing else can.
